## Non-Negotiable Naming Rules

- Never use `Lightning` alone in customer-facing copy. Use `Simpro Lightning`, `AroFlo Lightning`, `BigChange Lightning`, or `Simpro Group Lightning`.
- Use `Simpro Group Lightning` when speaking generically across the brand family.
- Use `JustAsk` as one word with both words capitalized. JustAsk is the conversational interface, not an agent.
- Use `Cooper` for the AI brain. Cooper is not the platform, not an agent, and not the product name.
- The four launch agents are exactly `FieldReady`, `JobReady`, `JobScribe`, and `JobBrief`.
- The plain customer-facing feature names are `Two-Way Messaging` and `GPS Time Tracking`.
- Product-marketing or Fred-voice contexts may use `DirectLine 2-Way Messaging` and `TrueTime GPS Timekeeping`, but do not mix the plain names and product names in the same asset.
- The Coming Specialists names are fixed: `FastCash`, `Delight`, `Pulse`, `Foresight`, `Forge`, `Swift`, `Sales Manager`, `Shield`, `Spark`, and `Precision`. Do not invent new specialist names.

## Architecture

The canonical architecture is:

> Your FSM is the heart. Cooper is the brain. Together, they are Simpro Group Lightning.

Use the brand-specific form when the heart brand is known:

- `Simpro Lightning`
- `AroFlo Lightning`
- `BigChange Lightning`

Core architecture rules:

- Simpro Group Lightning is an AI intelligence layer and platform upgrade, not a bolt-on, feature pack, add-on bundle, chatbot, or assistant.
- Cooper powers the agents and answers through JustAsk.
- JustAsk sits above the agent set as the interface to the whole brain. Do not list it as a fifth agent, business analyst, or team member.
- The agents are full-time roles the customer could not afford to hire, not features that merely help users.
- The platform is additive. Do not imply migration, rip-and-replace, swapping the FSM, or installing a new heart. The heart stays; the brain is added.
- The one acceptable heart-transplant metaphor is the inverse switching-cost point: changing FSMs entirely is painful. Do not use that metaphor to describe installing Simpro Group Lightning.

## Customer Descriptor

For Lightning-specific first-reference customer descriptions, default to `the field service trades`, `field service trade business`, or `field service trade operator`.

`The trades` is acceptable only as second-reference shorthand after the full scope has been established, in cadence-sensitive dignity lines, or when quoting customer language.

## Proof Stack And Economics

Use quantified Lightning claims only when they match this approved stack or have current source verification:

- Average field service trade business margin: 6%.
- North-star profit trajectory: 6% to 24%. Treat 24% as aspirational/top-quartile framing, not an industry-average promise.
- Execution-capacity frame: roughly 27 things to run well, roughly 7 done effectively, roughly 20 missing because the business cannot staff them.
- Scale proof: 24,000+ businesses, 450,000+ users, and 150 million+ jobs. Use lower customer/business/user figures only when quoting a dated approved source with attribution.
- Promotional launch pricing: 15% platform uplift on existing subscription through May 31.
- Standard list price after launch: 25% platform uplift.
- Price Lock Guarantee: annual increases capped at 3% plus in-country CPI.
- AI tax frame: AI-powered software is commonly framed as rising 20% to 25% per year.
- Three-year comparison frame: AI-powered platforms around 82% cumulative increase versus Simpro Group Lightning around 17% to 22%.
- Headcount frame: about $200,000 of operational capability for about $2,000 per year. Treat this as rough-order economics that varies by customer subscription.

Time-sensitive pricing, roadmap, and competitor claims must be checked before public publication.

## Story Model

Full-length Lightning assets should preserve this order when possible:

1. The 6% margin and execution-capacity problem.
2. AI as the economic shift that makes missing headcount practical.
3. Heart + brain: the existing FSM remains the heart, Cooper brings the brain.
4. What the customer gets: faster FSM improvement, digital workers, and JustAsk.
5. Price Lock and the AI tax.
6. Why now: the brain learns over time and the launch rate is time-bound.
7. Close on JustAsk or a concrete next step.

Short-form content should preserve the compact structure:

> [Brand] Lightning gives your FSM a brain. The brain changes everything: faster platform improvement, digital workers, and a business that can answer its own questions.

Use the JustAsk tagline only as:

> Don't Wonder - Just Ask.

Do not mutate it to include Cooper.

## Voice And Quality Gate

Lightning copy should be blunt, direct, proof-led, and respectful of the field service trades. Lead with numbers and operational outcomes, not generic AI enthusiasm.

Flag and revise any asset that:

- Uses `Lightning` without a brand prefix in customer-facing copy.
- Uses first-reference `the trades` instead of `the field service trades`.
- Treats JustAsk as an agent or peer of the four agents.
- Calls Cooper the platform or an agent.
- Claims Simpro Group Lightning replaces people instead of giving the business roles it could not afford to hire.
- Frames the offering as a bolt-on, add-on, chatbot, assistant, or feature bundle.
- Uses unsourced or inflated quantified claims.
- Uses competitor claims without current verification.
- Omits Price Lock in pricing or decision-stage content.
- Mixes Fred strategic register and Corey operational register in the same asset.
